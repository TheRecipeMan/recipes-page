When I was younger, I didn't really like macaroni and cheese that much. Now, I think I found a recipe that tastes good.

Cooking Information
Prep Time Cook Time Servings
35 mins 15 mins It could feed six people

Ingredients

  • 1 box of elbow macaroni noodles
  • 4 cups of milk, half-and-half or heavy cream
  • 4 tablespoons of butter (only applies if you are using milk or half-and-half).
  • 4 tablespoons of flour (only applies if you are using milk or half-and-half).
  • 16 oz of mild cheddar cheese.
  • 16 oz of sharp cheddar cheese.
  • 8 oz of muenster cheese.
  • 3 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp pepper
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder

Instructions

  1. Boil a pot of heavily salted water. Do not add oil.
  2. Put the elbow noodles in the pot and cook according to the package instructions.
  3. Strain the noodles.
  4. In a separate pot, melt your butter and add your flour to create a roux. Feel free to omit this step if you are using heavy cream.
  5. When the roux is successfully combined, add your milk.
  6. Add your velveeta cheese and two thirds of your combined other cheeses, but save some for later.
  7. Add your elbow noodles. Feel free to add some seasonings like pepper, salt or paprika.
  8. In a greased or buttered pan, add your macaroni. Use the remaining cheese to sprinkle on top.
  9. Bake on 400 degrees for 15 minutes. Enjoy!
